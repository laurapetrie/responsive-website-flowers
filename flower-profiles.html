Foxglove

Foxgloves are tall, stately plants native to Europe, western Asia and northwestern Africa. The flowers are tubular in shape, produced on a tall spike and vary in colour with species from purple to pink, white and yellow.

The best-known species is the common foxglove, Digitalis purpurea. This biennial is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its vivid flowers and various marks and spottings.

The generic epithet Digitalis is from the Latin digitus (finger).The name is recorded in Old English as 'foxes glofe/glofa' or 'fox's glove'. Over time, folk myths obscured the literal origins of the name, insinuating that foxes wore the flowers on their paws to silence their movements as they stealthily hunted their prey.

Hydrangea

Commonly named the hortensia, hydrangea is a genus of over 75 species of flowering plants native to Asia and the Americas. By far the greatest species diversity is in eastern Asia, notably China, Korea, and Japan.

The name is derived from Greek and means 'water vessel' due to the shape of its seed capsules. The flowers of hydrangea act as natural pH indicators, sporting blue flowers when the soil is acidic and pink when the soil is alkaline.

Hydrangeas are popular ornamental plants, grown for their large flowerheads, with Hydrangea macrophylla (meaning bigleaf hydrangea) being by far the most widely grown. It has over 600 named cultivars and can be broken up into two main categories; mophead and lacecap.

Lisianthus

Eustoma, commonly known in English as lisianthus or prairie gentian, is a small genus of plants in the gentian family. They are native to warm regions of the southern US, Mexico, Caribbean and northern South America. They are typically found in grasslands and in areas of disturbed ground.

They are herbaceous annuals, with bluish green, slightly succulent leaves and large funnel-shaped flowers growing on long straight stems. The flowers are either single or double-flowered and found in a wide variety of colours: pink, peach, purple, blue, white, brown and red.

The genus name Eustoma is a compound of the Greek prefix eu- 'good / pleasing' and -stoma , 'mouth' - hence '( having a ) pleasing mouth'.

Peony

The peony or paeony is a flowering plant in the genus Paeonia and are native to Asia, Europe and Western North America. They have large, often fragrant flowers, in colors ranging from purple and pink to red, white, peach or yellow.

They are popular garden plants and are also sold as cut flowers on a large scale. They have a small window of availability from late spring to early summer, which accounts for their high demand for wedding flowers at this time.

The peony is named after Paeon, a student of Asclepius, the Greek god of medicine and healing. In Greek mythology, when Asclepius became jealous of his pupil, Zeus saved Paeon from the wrath of Asclepius by turning him into the peony flower.

Ranunculus

Ranunculus asiaticus, the Persian buttercup, is a species native to the eastern Mediterranean region in southwestern Asia, southeastern Europe, and northeastern Africa.

Double-flowered forms are a popular ornamental plant in gardens, and widely used in floristry. The italian cultivated Cloni ranunculus has many petals resembling a mini peony. They come in a variety of colours from white, green, pink, yellow, red, orange and peach with some having multicolored, ruffled petals.

They prefer a cooler climate and so as a cut flower are usually available from December until the end of May.

Rose

The majority of ornamental roses are hybrids that have been bred for their flowers and perfume. They have been cultivated for millennia, with the earliest known to date from at least 500 BC in Mediterranean countries, Persia, and China.

The long cultural history of the rose has led to it being used often as a symbol in mythology and art. It has closely been associated with Aphrodite in ancient Greece and the Virgin Mary following the Christianization of the Roamn Empire. Roses are a favored subject in art and appear in portraits, illustrations, on stamps, as ornaments or as architectural elements.

The David Austen Juliet Rose is one of the most expensive roses in the world. They are distinguished by their full cups, peachy scent and beautiful apricot colored petals.
